Kids Dental Services

Everything your child's smile needs to thrive.

From the first baby tooth to teenage orthodontics — comprehensive specialist care at every stage.

🔍
Check-Up & Cleaning
Regular dental check-ups and professional cleaning help catch issues early and establish a healthy routine. First visit is always free for new child patients.
🛡️
Dental Sealants
Protective coatings applied to the grooves of back teeth — highly effective at preventing cavities in children. Painless, quick and one of the best investments in your child's dental health.
💧
Fluoride Treatment
Professional fluoride applications strengthen enamel and dramatically reduce cavity risk. Recommended at every 6-month check-up for children at all ages.
🦷
Cavity Treatment (Fillings)
Gentle cavity treatment with tooth-coloured composite fillings. Our specialist team is experienced in treating children with minimal anxiety and maximum comfort.
😬
Interceptive Orthodontics
Early treatment during mixed dentition (ages 6–12) can guide jaw growth and prevent severe crowding. Dr. Achla Goel identifies orthodontic issues early, when they're easiest and cheapest to correct.
🎮
Dental Anxiety Management
Specialised behaviour management techniques for anxious children — Tell-Show-Do, distraction therapy and a calm, child-designed environment. No child is rushed, ever.

Dental Care by Age

The right care at every stage.

Your child's dental needs evolve as they grow. Here's what to expect at each stage.

Age 1–5
Baby Teeth & First Habits
  • First dental visit by age 1
  • Brushing technique guidance for parents
  • Cavity risk assessment
  • Fluoride varnish application
  • Diet counselling for dental health
  • Thumb-sucking and dummy guidance
Age 6–12
Mixed Dentition & Growth
  • Monitoring tooth eruption sequence
  • Dental sealants for back teeth
  • Cavity treatment if needed
  • Space maintainers for early tooth loss
  • Early orthodontic assessment
  • Interceptive treatment if indicated
Age 12–17
Permanent Teeth & Orthodontics
  • Full orthodontic assessment
  • Braces or clear aligners options
  • Wisdom tooth monitoring
  • Sports mouthguard fitting
  • Whitening readiness assessment
  • Oral hygiene coaching for teens

Parent Questions

Everything parents ask us about kids dentistry.

The IDA recommends a child's first dental visit by age 1, or within 6 months of the first tooth appearing — whichever comes first. Early visits let us monitor development, guide parents on brushing and diet, apply protective fluoride and most importantly, build a positive relationship between your child and dental care before any problems arise.
Yes, 100%. The first dental visit for new child patients at Bite Dental Clinic is completely free. It includes a check-up, basic assessment and parent counselling. There's no hidden charge and no obligation for any further treatment. We want your child's first dental experience to be positive — and that means no financial surprises for you either.
This is exactly what Dr. Achla Goel specialises in. Dental anxiety in children is extremely common — and manageable with the right approach. We use Tell-Show-Do (explaining before doing), distraction techniques and a calm, child-friendly environment. We never rush a child and never proceed until they feel comfortable. Most parents are amazed at how quickly their previously-terrified child begins to enjoy coming.
Dental sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the grooves of permanent back teeth (molars) — the areas most prone to cavities. Research shows sealants can reduce cavity risk by up to 80% in treated teeth. They're quick, painless and highly cost-effective compared to filling a cavity later. Dr. Achla Goel will assess your child and recommend sealants based on their specific cavity risk profile.
Every 6 months is the standard recommendation for children. Regular visits allow us to catch issues early (when they're smallest and cheapest to treat), apply preventive treatments and monitor development. Children with higher cavity risk may benefit from more frequent visits — which we'll guide you on at your child's check-up.
